Frequently Asked Questions

To securely surf the Internet, you know you need a VPN service provider. Please review our FAQ for other important questions and answers.

What is a VPN?

A VPN is a “Virtual Private Network”. A VPN protects your privacy and prevents others from monitoring and controlling your online communications and browsing activity.

How does a VPN work?

A VPN works by establishing a ‘secure and encrypted’ connection, between your computer and the internet; providing a private tunnel for your data and communications, while you use the internet connected networks.

How does a VPN help the user?

By encrypting your data and hiding your IP address, a VPN also helps to protect your online identity and prevents your activities from being tracked. With a VPN, your location stays private and you can switch freely between multiple global VPN server locations.

Is VPN data encrypted?

When you use a VPN, your data connection is encrypted, so ISPs, governments, NSA spies, and other third parties cannot see which websites you visit, what you download, or whether you use services such as Skype or a P2P application. Still, you need to trust your VPN provider.
